## Section 1: Assignment Brief & Criteria Explained

### Your Task

Investigate recruitment and selection at a LARGE business (250+ employees).

You will write a report explaining:
- HOW your chosen business recruits and selects employees
- WHY they use the methods they do
- How they follow the LAW and ETHICAL practices
- How EFFECTIVE their recruitment is for business success

Word count: 2,000 - 2,500 words

### Assessment Criteria — In Plain English

**P1: Explain how a large business recruits and selects, giving reasons for their processes**

What this means:
- Describe the recruitment methods your business uses (internal/external)
- Describe the selection methods they use (interviews, tests, etc.)
- Explain WHY they use each method — give REASONS
- Include examples from your research

Key word: EXPLAIN = Don't just describe, give REASONS using 'because', 'this means that', 'the reason is'

**P2: Explain how and why a business adheres to recruitment processes which are ethical and comply with current employment law**

What this means:
- Explain which LAWS they follow (Equality Act, Data Protection, etc.)
- Give EXAMPLES of how they comply with each law
- Explain their ETHICAL practices (fairness, transparency, diversity, etc.)
- Explain WHY following law and ethics is important

Key word: EXPLAIN = Show HOW they comply and WHY it matters

**M1: Analyse the different recruitment methods used in a selected business**

What this means:
- For each recruitment method, explain the ADVANTAGES
- For each recruitment method, explain the DISADVANTAGES
- Explain WHY each advantage/disadvantage matters for the business
- Compare different methods — which works better for which roles?

Key word: ANALYSE = Break down into advantages AND disadvantages, explain WHY they matter

**D1: Evaluate the recruitment processes used and how they contribute to the success of the selected business**

What this means:
- Make JUDGEMENTS — how effective is their recruitment overall?
- Link recruitment to BUSINESS SUCCESS (productivity, costs, retention, reputation)
- Weigh up the positives and negatives
- Reach a CONCLUSION based on evidence

Key word: EVALUATE = Judge effectiveness, link to success, weigh up evidence, reach a conclusion

### What Does Each Grade Look Like?

PASS: EXPLAIN with reasons (What + Why). Example: "Tesco uses online recruitment because it reaches many candidates quickly and cheaply."

MERIT: ANALYSE advantages and disadvantages. Example: "An advantage of online recruitment is wide reach. However, a disadvantage is it may attract unsuitable applicants."

DISTINCTION: EVALUATE effectiveness and link to success. Example: "Overall, Tesco's recruitment is effective because the cost savings contribute to competitive pricing, although..."

### Building Up Through the Grades

You CANNOT skip levels!

- To get MERIT, you must FIRST achieve all PASS criteria
- To get DISTINCTION, you must FIRST achieve PASS and MERIT

This means your assignment should:
1. EXPLAIN everything thoroughly (Pass)
2. THEN add analysis of advantages/disadvantages (Merit)
3. THEN add evaluation and judgements (Distinction)

Think of it as layers — each grade adds more depth to your work.

## Section 2: Assignment Planning Template

### Section-by-Section Planning

INTRODUCTION (~150 words): Name of business and sector, brief background (size, locations, what they do), what the report will cover.

SECTION 1: RECRUITMENT PROCESS (~500 words) — For P1: WHY the business recruits (reasons for vacancies), internal recruitment methods + examples, external recruitment methods + examples, recruitment documents (job descriptions, person specs, adverts), REASONS for each method.

SECTION 2: SELECTION PROCESS (~400 words) — For P1: Selection methods used (interviews, tests, assessment centres, etc.), technology used in recruitment (online applications, ATS, video interviews), REASONS for each method.

SECTION 3: LEGAL & ETHICAL (~400 words) — For P2: Equality Act 2010 — how they comply + example, Data Protection — how they comply + example, other relevant laws, ethical practices (fairness, transparency, diversity, etc.), WHY following law and ethics is important for the business.

SECTION 4: ANALYSIS (~400 words) — For M1: Advantages of their recruitment methods, disadvantages of their recruitment methods, WHY each advantage/disadvantage matters, comparison — which methods work best for which roles?

SECTION 5: EVALUATION (~400 words) — For D1: Overall judgement — how effective is their recruitment? Link to business success (productivity, costs, retention, reputation), weighing up positives vs negatives, supported conclusion based on evidence.

CONCLUSION (~100 words): Summary of key findings, final judgement on effectiveness, brief reflection on how recruitment contributes to success.

## Section 3: Writing Frames

### PASS Level Writing Frame — Heavy Scaffolding

Use this frame for P1 and P2. Copy the sentence starters and fill in the gaps.

Writing Frame: Explaining a Recruitment Method (P1):
"[Business name] uses _____ as a recruitment method. This method involves _____. They use this method because _____. This is appropriate for [business name] because _____. For example, _____."

Writing Frame: Explaining a Selection Method (P1):
"When selecting candidates, [business name] uses _____. This selection method involves _____. The reason they use this method is because _____. This helps them find the right candidates because _____."

Writing Frame: Explaining Legal Compliance (P2):
"[Business name] complies with the _____ Act. This law requires businesses to _____. [Business name] follows this law by _____. For example, they _____. Following this law is important because _____."

Writing Frame: Explaining Ethical Practices (P2):
"[Business name] demonstrates ethical recruitment through _____. This means they _____. They do this because _____. An example of this is _____. Being ethical in recruitment helps the business because _____."

### MERIT Level Writing Frame — Moderate Scaffolding

Use this frame for M1. You need to analyse advantages AND disadvantages.

Writing Frame: Analysing Recruitment Methods (M1):

PARAGRAPH 1 — Advantages: "An advantage of [method] is _____. This benefits [business name] because _____. Another advantage is _____. This means that _____."

PARAGRAPH 2 — Disadvantages: "However, a disadvantage of [method] is _____. This could be a problem because _____. Additionally, _____. This matters because _____."

PARAGRAPH 3 — Overall: "Despite these drawbacks, [method] is appropriate for [business] because _____."

Merit Analysis — Useful Phrases:
- For advantages: 'An advantage of this method is...', 'This benefits the business because...', 'A strength of this approach is...', 'This is effective because...'
- For disadvantages: 'However, a disadvantage is...', 'On the other hand...', 'A potential weakness is...', 'This could be problematic because...'
- For linking: 'This matters for [business] because...', 'The impact of this is...', 'As a result...', 'This affects business success by...'

### DISTINCTION Level Writing Frame — Light Scaffolding

Use this frame for D1. You need to evaluate and make judgements.

Writing Frame: Evaluating Recruitment (D1):

PARAGRAPH 1 — Overall Judgement: State how effective their recruitment is overall. Identify the most significant strengths.

PARAGRAPH 2 — Link to Business Success: Explain HOW recruitment contributes to their success. Consider: productivity, costs, staff retention, reputation, competitive advantage.

PARAGRAPH 3 — Weighing Up: Acknowledge limitations or areas for improvement. Use 'However, it could be argued that...'

PARAGRAPH 4 — Conclusion: Reach a final judgement based on evidence. Use 'Overall, the evidence suggests...' Make a clear statement about effectiveness.

Distinction Evaluation — Useful Phrases:
- For judging effectiveness: 'This is highly effective because...', 'This demonstrates good practice because...', 'This contributes to success by...'
- For linking to success: 'This contributes to business success because...', 'The impact on [productivity/costs/retention] is...', 'This gives them a competitive advantage because...'
- For weighing up: 'However, it could be argued that...', 'A limitation of this approach is...', 'On balance...'
- For concluding: 'Overall, the evidence suggests...', 'In conclusion, [business]'s recruitment is effective because...', 'The strengths outweigh the weaknesses because...'

### What Does Meeting Criteria Actually Look Like?

PASS Example — This MEETS criteria: "Tesco uses their company careers website to recruit for store-based roles. This method allows candidates to search for jobs by location and apply directly online. They use this method because it is cost-effective compared to recruitment agencies, and it allows them to reach a large number of candidates quickly. This is appropriate for Tesco because they have over 3,000 stores and need to fill many similar positions regularly. For example, their website currently advertises over 500 customer assistant roles across the UK." WHY IT WORKS: Names the method, describes what it involves, explains WHY with reasons, gives a specific example.

PASS Example — This DOESN'T meet criteria: "Tesco uses their website to recruit people. They also use job boards. They interview people and check references." WHY IT FAILS: Only describes — no explanation of WHY. No reasons given. No specific examples. Too brief.

MERIT Example — This MEETS criteria: "An advantage of using their careers website is that Tesco can control their employer branding and ensure consistent messaging to candidates. This benefits the business because it helps attract candidates who already identify with Tesco's values, potentially reducing turnover costs. However, a disadvantage is that only candidates who are actively looking for jobs at Tesco will find the website. This means they may miss 'passive' candidates who would be interested but aren't actively searching. This could limit the talent pool for more specialist roles." WHY IT WORKS: Identifies specific advantage AND disadvantage. Explains WHY each matters for the business.

DISTINCTION Example — This MEETS criteria: "Overall, Tesco's recruitment approach is highly effective in supporting their business model. The combination of high-volume online recruitment for store roles and structured assessment for management positions ensures they can maintain service levels while developing internal talent. This directly contributes to business success by keeping recruitment costs low while maintaining quality. Their reported staff turnover of 20% is below the retail industry average, suggesting their selection processes identify suitable candidates. However, the emphasis on efficiency may sometimes compromise candidate experience. Glassdoor reviews indicate some applicants feel the process is impersonal. On balance, the evidence suggests Tesco's recruitment is effective for their needs. The cost savings and consistent staffing outweigh the limitations, contributing to their position as the UK's largest retailer." WHY IT WORKS: Makes judgements, links to success with evidence, acknowledges limitations, reaches a supported conclusion.

## Section 6: Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Describing without explaining: Just listing what they do. Instead, explain WHY they do it using 'because', 'this means that'. Affects P1/P2.
- No specific examples: "They use interviews". Instead, "They use panel interviews with 3 interviewers, including..." Affects All.
- Listing without analysis: "Advantages: cheap, fast, wide reach". Instead, explain each advantage and WHY it helps the business. Affects M1.
- No disadvantages: Only writing about positives. Every method has drawbacks — include them! Affects M1.
- No judgement: Finishing without concluding. End with "Overall, this is effective because..." Affects D1.
- Not linking to success: Describing methods in isolation. Explain how each method contributes to business success. Affects D1.
- Copying from websites: Pasting text without changing it. Paraphrase in your own words and reference the source. Affects All.
- Generic content: Writing about recruitment generally. Every point should relate to YOUR specific business. Affects All.
- Too short: Not enough detail or examples. Aim for 2,000-2,500 words with detailed explanations. Affects All.
- No bibliography: Forgetting to list sources. Include a reference list at the end. Affects All.

Quick Fixes:
- IF your work is too descriptive → Add 'because...' after every main point
- IF you have no examples → Go back to your research and add specific evidence
- IF your analysis is one-sided → Add 'However, a disadvantage is...'
- IF you have no conclusion → Add a final paragraph starting 'Overall, the evidence suggests...'
- IF it's too short → Expand each point with more detail and examples
- IF it's generic → Search and replace general statements with specific ones about YOUR business
