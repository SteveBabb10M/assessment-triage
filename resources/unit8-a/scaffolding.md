# Unit 8 Assignment A — Student Scaffolding Guide

## Structure Your Work

### For Pass (P1 and P2)
Use the DESCRIBE → EXPLAIN structure:
- State what the method/law is
- Then explain HOW the business uses it
- Then explain WHY the business uses it (link to business goals)

Example structure for each recruitment method:
1. What is [method]? (1-2 sentences)
2. How does [business] use this method? (describe the process)
3. Why does [business] use this method? (give reasons linked to business needs)

### For Merit (M1)
Use PEEL paragraphs:
- **P**oint: State which recruitment method you are analysing
- **E**vidence: Give specific evidence from your chosen business
- **E**xplain: Analyse WHY this advantage/disadvantage matters for this specific business
- **L**ink: Link back to how this affects the business's recruitment effectiveness

Don't just list advantages and disadvantages — explain the MECHANISM:
- NOT: "Agencies are expensive" ✗
- YES: "Recruitment agencies charge 15-20% of the candidate's first year salary. For Amazon, which recruits over 500,000 workers annually, this represents a significant cost that must be weighed against the time saved in the hiring process and the quality of candidates the agency can pre-screen." ✓

### For Distinction (D1)
Use the evaluation framework:
1. Opening judgement: "Overall, [business]'s recruitment approach is [effective/partially effective/etc] because..."
2. Evidence FOR: Present the strongest arguments supporting the recruitment approach
3. Evidence AGAINST: Present the limitations or weaknesses (counter-argument)
4. Balanced conclusion: Weigh both sides and reach a final judgement
5. Link to business success: How does recruitment contribute to the company's performance?

## Key Vocabulary to Use
- Recruitment vs Selection (know the difference)
- Internal recruitment, external recruitment
- Equality Act 2010, protected characteristics
- Ethical recruitment, fair hiring, equal opportunities
- Cost-effectiveness, time-to-hire, retention rates
- Workforce planning, skills gap

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't confuse recruitment (attracting candidates) with selection (choosing candidates)
- Don't discuss general business laws — focus on recruitment-specific legislation
- Don't just list advantages/disadvantages without explaining why they matter for YOUR business
- Don't write a generic evaluation — use specific evidence from your case study
- Don't forget the Equality Act 2010 — this is the most important law for P2
