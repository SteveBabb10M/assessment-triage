# Task 1.1 — Investigate the Project Brief: Marking Grid

**Duration:** 8 hours (+0.5 hours reading time)
**Total marks:** 20
**Number of marking grids:** 3

---

## Required Evidence

- Research outcomes summary table
- PESTLE & SWOT analysis
- Outline of two possible solutions
- Cost benefit analysis with selected solution clearly identified

---

## Grid 1: AO1 (Planning) + AO3 (Techniques & Resources) — 6 marks

### Band 1 (1–2 marks)
Some evidence of a planned approach to research, though not always logical. Some areas of the brief researched, but findings may be limited and not always captured clearly or related to the brief. Some evidence of relevant techniques and resources used, but limited sources considered. Resources provide limited support for planning research.

### Band 2 (3–4 marks)
Research approach clearly planned and organised, undertaken logically, covering most areas outlined by the brief. Findings clearly captured and mostly relevant. Range of relevant techniques and resources used and referenced, with different sources considered. Suitable resources selected and used to support planning most of the time.

### Band 3 (5–6 marks)
Clear evidence of a detailed, methodical and thorough approach. Research and information gathering consistent and logical, reflecting full requirements of the brief. Findings clearly captured with a high level of relevance. Range of techniques and resources reviewed and compared to find the most relevant, including non-traditional sources. Resources used to optimally support planning, with evidence of how they shape the response.

---

## Grid 2: AO2a (Core Knowledge) — 6 marks

### Band 1 (1–2 marks)
Elements of core knowledge evidenced through research. Comprehension may have gaps or misunderstanding in relation to brief. Technical accuracy is limited. Core knowledge in proposals shows some inaccuracies. Limited attempts to justify and support judgements with gaps in reasoning. Concepts not always clear or accurate.

### Band 2 (3–4 marks)
Core knowledge specifically evident through research and detailed in relation to different elements of the project brief. Some connections evident between elements of core knowledge. Mostly technically accurate. Proposals mostly accurate. Judgements logical with some detailed reasoning. Concepts explained clearly and accurately.

### Band 3 (5–6 marks)
Core knowledge specifically evident through research, effectively taking into account elements of the project brief. Connections between elements exploited to strengthen analysis and demonstrate understanding. Consistently technically accurate. Proposals consistently accurate. Judgements logical with comprehensive reasoning throughout. Concepts effectively presented clearly and accurately.

---

## Grid 3: AO2b (Core Skills) + AO4a (Maths Skills) — 8 marks

**Maths competencies assessed:** MC2 Estimating/calculating/error spotting, MC5 Processing data, MC6 Understanding data and risk, MC8 Communicating using mathematics, MC9 Costing a project, MC10 Optimising work processes

### Band 1 (1–2 marks)
Basic level of research with limited outcomes identified. Basic information gathering with basic PESTLE/SWOT analysis. Cost benefit calculations attempted with limited correct methodology and errors evident. Limited analysis presented. Limited cost benefit analysis focused just around figures provided in the brief.

### Band 2 (3–4 marks)
Research undertaken with some outcomes clearly identified. Limited information gathering including PESTLE/SWOT in some detail. Cost benefit calculations mostly well-attempted with correct methodology mostly demonstrated and some errors. Some basic analysis conducted based on findings.

### Band 3 (5–6 marks)
Research detailed with outcomes clearly identified and accurately referenced. Detailed information gathering including detailed PESTLE/SWOT analysis. Cost benefit calculations completed using correct methodology with minor errors. Detailed analysis conducted. Detailed cost benefit analysis includes figures from the brief, research, and other sources.

### Band 4 (7–8 marks)
Systematic and comprehensive research with outcomes coherently and accurately referenced. Comprehensive information gathering including comprehensive PESTLE/SWOT analysis. Cost benefit calculations completed accurately using correct methodology with fully accurate calculations. Detailed analysis with clear rationale and justifications for conclusions drawn.

---

## Indicative Content — Typical Evidence

### Planning and Research (AO1 & AO3)
- Selection of techniques and resources — research and application of PESTLE and SWOT
- Research around different approaches relevant to the brief topic and how these are used in similar organisations
- Research around how processes are impacted by structure, people, management, stakeholder/customer needs and location
- Mixture of quantitative and qualitative research
- Research into PESTLE factors driving need for efficiencies
- Internal drivers via SWOT analysis
- List of sources or referenced materials

### Core Knowledge (AO2a)
- Business context, project and change management, business behaviour
- Research coverage, knowledge of PESTLE and SWOT and information gathering
- Knowledge seen across the PESTLE and SWOT in relation to the project

### Core Skills and Maths (AO2b & AO4)
- Application of research as part of core skills
- Detailed options for two solutions
- Cost benefit analysis (not expecting ROI) considering advantages and disadvantages of each proposed solution, impact on organisation versus costs when considering budget in brief
- Cost expectations — use of costings from a mix of brief and research, costings captured for different solutions
- Benefits — used costings to link advantages and disadvantages to support selection of one option
- Assessment of financial viability of different options
- Comparison and analysis of proposed options and justification of decisions made
- Collated information and resources from research
- Use of decision-making matrix for comparing options
- Undertaking cost-benefit analysis and application to the brief
- Use of risk analysis and application to the brief, considering risk mitigation
- Consideration of wider options, including details of information that would be required but has not yet been possible to source
