# T Level M&A ESP — Quick Assessment Reference for Tasks 1.1 and 1.2

**For use by:** Catherine Pennington / AAP system
**Purpose:** Quick-reference guide for assessing student submissions against ESP criteria

---

## Task 1.1: Investigate the Project Brief (20 marks)

### Checklist — What Must Be Present

- [ ] PESTLE analysis (all six factors addressed)
- [ ] SWOT analysis (four quadrants)
- [ ] Research outcomes summary table
- [ ] Two possible solutions outlined
- [ ] Cost benefit analysis for both solutions
- [ ] Selected solution clearly identified with justification

### What Separates the Bands

| Aspect | Band 1 (Low) | Band 2 (Mid) | Band 3–4 (High) |
|--------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Research approach | Some evidence, not always logical | Clearly planned and organised | Detailed, methodical, thorough |
| PESTLE/SWOT | Basic, limited detail | Conducted in some detail | Comprehensive and detailed |
| Sources | Limited sources | Different sources considered | Non-traditional sources included |
| Link to brief | Findings not always related | Mostly relevant | High level of relevance |
| Core knowledge | Gaps or misunderstanding | Mostly accurate, some connections | Consistently accurate, connections exploited |
| Cost benefit | Limited methodology, errors | Mostly correct, some errors | Accurate methodology, minor/no errors |
| Analysis of options | Limited analysis | Some basic analysis | Clear rationale and justifications |

### Key Differentiators for High Marks
- Research goes BEYOND the brief (external sources, statistics, real-world examples)
- PESTLE/SWOT factors are explicitly linked back to the project context
- Two solutions are genuinely different (not just variations)
- Cost benefit includes figures from research, not just from the brief
- Selected solution justified with reference to brief requirements AND research findings

## Task 1.2: Project Initiation Document — PID (17 marks)

### Checklist — What Must Be Present

- [ ] Project information (name, date, author)
- [ ] Background and scope
- [ ] Project rationale (why this solution)
- [ ] Project aims and objectives
- [ ] Budget / anticipated costs with breakdown
- [ ] Project management team / reporting structure
- [ ] Key stakeholders with roles and responsibilities
- [ ] Project deliverables with formats and target dates
- [ ] Risk register with levels and mitigations
- [ ] Quality management method
- [ ] Project evaluation method

### What Separates the Bands

| Aspect | Band 1 (Low) | Band 2 (Mid) | Band 3–4 (High) |
|--------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Structure | Lacks order and organisation | Some structure, clearly organised | Comprehensive structure, methodical |
| Meets brief | Limited at meeting requirements | Mostly meets requirements | Fully meets or exceeds requirements |
| Justification | Limited justification for choices | Some detail to justify | Fully justified, most effective choices |
| Core knowledge | Basic details, gaps in comprehension | Some detail, mostly clear | Comprehensive, coherent rationale |
| Links to research | Basic links, some inaccuracies | Clear and accurate links | Thorough, detailed, exploited to strengthen arguments |
| Information quality | Not always clear or coherent | Mostly clear and coherent | Comprehensive with coherent rationale |

### Key Differentiators for High Marks
- PID is comprehensive — every section completed with substantive detail
- Clear logical link between the solution from 1.1 and the project plan
- Risks are specific to the project (not generic) with graduated severity
- Stakeholders include both internal AND external parties with specific responsibilities
- Budget breakdown clearly maps to project components
- Evaluation method is concrete and measurable (not just "we will evaluate")
- Deliverables have realistic timescales with justified ordering/sequencing

## Common Weaknesses to Look For

### Task 1.1
- PESTLE that lists factors generically without linking to the specific project/organisation
- SWOT that is too brief or doesn't draw on evidence
- Only one solution considered, or two very similar solutions
- Cost benefit that only uses figures from the brief (not from independent research)
- No justification for why the selected solution is best
- Missing research summary table
- No referenced sources

### Task 1.2
- PID sections left blank or with minimal content
- Generic risks not specific to the project
- No risk mitigation strategies
- Budget not broken down or linked to project components
- Stakeholders listed without roles/responsibilities
- No evaluation method or quality management approach
- Deliverables without target dates
- No logical sequencing of project phases
- PID disconnected from the solution selected in Task 1.1

## Authenticity Indicators for ESP Submissions

Given that the ESP is completed under controlled conditions (timed, supervised), submissions analysed through AAP should be considered in this context:

**Expected characteristics of genuine ESP work:**
- Consistent writing quality throughout (students are writing under time pressure)
- References to specific brief details (the brief is unique per series)
- Costings that attempt to use brief-provided figures
- Some errors or inconsistencies (common under timed conditions)
- Personal voice in rationale and justification sections
- PID that follows logically from Task 1.1 solution

**Potential concerns:**
- Submission quality dramatically different from Task 1.1 to Task 1.2
- Generic content that could apply to any brief (not the specific one set)
- Overly polished language inconsistent with timed conditions
- Content that doesn't reference the specific organisation/scenario in the brief
- Perfect formatting/structure without any of the roughness expected under exam conditions
