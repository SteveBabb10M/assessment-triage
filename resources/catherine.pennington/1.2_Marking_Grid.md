# Task 1.2 — Project Initiation Document (PID): Marking Grid

**Duration:** 3.5 hours
**Total marks:** 17
**Number of marking grids:** 2

---

## Required Evidence

- Project Initiation Document

---

## Grid 1: AO1 (Planning) + AO3 (Techniques & Resources) — 8 marks

### Band 1 (1–2 marks)
Some evidence of a planned approach to meeting the project brief but lacks order, organisation and structure. The approach is limited at meeting the brief requirements. Limited justification for the selection of techniques, resources (e.g., human resource requirements, financials), methods and approaches. Choices not always the most effective or appropriate.

### Band 2 (3–4 marks)
Approach to planning clearly organised with some attempt to follow a structure. Mostly meets the requirements of the brief. Techniques and/or resources, methods and approaches selected respond to the brief with some detail used to justify how they meet the brief. Choices mostly accurate and appropriate.

### Band 3 (5–6 marks)
Approach to planning detailed, clearly organised and follows a defined structure. Meets the requirements of the brief. Selection of techniques, resources, methods and approaches are accurate and appropriately supported with clear justifications. Choices accurate and appropriate.

### Band 4 (7–8 marks)
Approach to planning shows clear evidence of a methodical and thorough approach, following a comprehensive structure. Brief requirements considered throughout. Detailed and fully justified approach to the selection of techniques, resources, methods and approaches. Choices are the most effective, accurate and appropriate for the prescribed brief.

---

## Grid 2: AO2a (Core Knowledge) + AO2b (Core Skills) — 9 marks

### Band 1 (1–3 marks)
Development of a PID with basic details. Some evidence of a considered approach in collating information but may not be clearly planned. Basic information largely provided but lacks coherence and may not always relate to the brief. Most elements of core knowledge evidenced but comprehension may have gaps or misunderstanding. Does not always fully meet brief requirements. Basic links to application of core knowledge to justify and support project scoping, but with some gaps or inaccuracies in reasoning that may not always relate to the brief.

### Band 2 (4–6 marks)
Development of a PID in some detail. Approach to collation of information clearly planned and organised with some evidence of a logical approach. Information provided is clearly related to the project and is clear and coherent. Core knowledge specifically evidenced and applied in relation to different elements of the project brief to meet some of the brief requirements. Clear links to application of core knowledge to justify and support project scoping. Clear and accurate links from research demonstrated through project scoping. Judgements logical with comprehensive lines of reasoning that link to the brief.

### Band 3 (7–9 marks)
Development of a comprehensive PID. Approach to collation of information is logical and effective, presenting a clear and organised approach effectively linked to the project. Level of information comprehensive in its linkages to the project, with coherent rationale and explanations. Core knowledge evidenced effectively and applied consistently throughout, effectively linking elements of the brief. Thorough and detailed links to application of core knowledge exploited to strengthen arguments and demonstrate understanding. Thorough and detailed links from research demonstrated and are accurate. Judgements logical and link effectively to the brief with comprehensive lines of reasoning.

## Indicative Content — Typical Evidence

### What a PID Should Include

- **Project management information** to aid the coordination of resources within time and resource constraints and to meet the organisation's needs
- **Project management phases** with consideration of coordination of resources, including who to involve and when (internal and external stakeholders)
- **Scope of the project** — pre-determine project aims and objectives, intended tasks or identified problems without exceeding time, cost and quality constraints
- **Key project deliverables** — format and target completion date for each deliverable or project stage
- **Impact identification** — opportunities/threats of implementing the proposed solution (e.g., financial implications in relation to the brief)
- **Risk assessment** — details and risk mitigation evidence for each risk. Types of risk may include: staff availability, costs of implementing technology, multiple locations, third-party reliability
- **Timescale consideration** — high-level consideration of timescales and how these will be met using recognised project management and/or time management tools
- **Budget management** — consideration of budget outlined in the project and how this will be maintained and regularly checked
- **Evaluation and quality management** — consideration of how the project will be evaluated and quality managed to measure project outcomes against objectives and aims

### Skills Demonstrated

- Produce a PID document
- Evidence of planning skills together with the ability to identify and deploy appropriate tools and methodologies
- Evidence of effective information skills in the production of the PID, tailored to the audience in line with the organisation's needs and project brief

## PID Template Structure (as used in assessment)

The PID template typically includes the following sections:

1. **Project Information** — Project name, date, author/project manager, version
2. **Document Approval** — Date, name, signature
3. **Background and Scope** — Context of the project and its boundaries
4. **Project Rationale** — Why this specific pathway/solution was chosen
5. **Project Aims and Objectives** — What the project intends to achieve
6. **Budget / Anticipated Costs** — Total budget and breakdown
7. **Project Management Team** — Roles and reporting lines
8. **Project Key Stakeholders** — Names, roles, responsibilities
9. **Project Deliverables** — What will be delivered, format, target dates
10. **Risks** — Risk number, details, level (Low/Med/High), mitigation
11. **Quality Management Method** — How quality will be maintained
12. **Project Evaluation Method** — How the project's success will be measured
