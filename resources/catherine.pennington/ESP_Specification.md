# T Level Management and Administration — Employer Set Project (ESP) Specification

**Qualification:** City & Guilds 8715 — T Level Technical Qualification in Management and Administration
**Component:** 8715-033 Employer-Set Project
**Assessment:** Externally set and externally marked by City & Guilds
**Total marks:** 100
**Total duration:** 25 hours across all tasks
**Grading:** A*–E plus Ungraded (U)
**Assessment window:** March–May annually (Summer series); October (Autumn series)

---

## ESP Overview

The Employer-Set Project is a multi-task assessment requiring learners to respond to a well-defined, real industry-style brief. The brief is complex and non-routine, requiring use of relevant maths, English and digital skills. It draws on the common core knowledge that sits across all specialisms for Management and Administration.

The project is made up of six tasks, all related to the same employer-set brief:

| Task | Title | Duration | Marks |
|------|-------|----------|-------|
| 1.1 | Investigate the project brief | 8 hours (+0.5 hrs reading) | 20 |
| 1.2 | Project Initiation Document (PID) | 3.5 hours | 17 |
| 1.3 | Project plan | 4 hours | 17 |
| 1.4 | Presentation | 3.5 hours | 19 |
| 2.1 | Collaborative problem-solving | 2.5 hours | 12 |
| 2.2 | Evaluation | 3.5 hours | 15 |

## Relevant Core Content Areas

The ESP samples knowledge drawn from across the core content, but all versions will cover:

- **Business Contexts** (Section 1 of core content)
- **Project and Change Management** (Section 6 of core content)
- **Business Behaviours** (Section 7 of core content)

### Key Core Knowledge for ESP Tasks

**Business Context** — Types of organisations, organisational objectives and strategies, stakeholder management, impacts of digital technologies, organisational culture and values, communication methods and channels.

**Project and Change Management** — Drivers of organisational change, SWOT and PESTLE analysis, continuous improvement and innovation, change management theories (Kotter, Nudge, Lewin, McKinsey 7S, ADKAR, Kubler-Ross), project management methodologies (Six Sigma, PRINCE, Agile, SCRUM), project management tools (dashboards, Gantt charts, PID, plan-on-a-page, critical flow analysis), research and evaluation methods.

**Business Behaviours** — Good communication and adapting styles, self-management approaches, adapting behaviour to organisational expectations, acting with integrity and building trust.

---

## General Marking Approach

- Candidate evidence must be marked based on what has been submitted
- If evidence is incomplete, marks are based only on what is submitted
- Errors carried forward from one task to another should only be penalised once
- Each task assessed within the constraints of its own marking grid
- Indicative content is not exhaustive; candidates do not need to cover all points
- Band descriptors should be read as a whole to form an overall judgement
- Where a range of marks exists per band, the marker confirms a final mark based on level of alignment with the descriptor

---

## Core Component Grade Descriptors (Summary)

**Grade A:** Comprehensive understanding of business contexts. Comprehensive links between relevant contexts. Thorough comprehension of business and management concepts. Meticulous approach to tool and methodology selection. Range of communication strategies adapted to audience. High degree of accuracy. Critical evaluation of own performance.

**Grade E:** Limited understanding of some business contexts. Limited links that may be superficial. Limited ability to comprehend concepts. Limited consideration when analysing briefs. Limited range of communication strategies. Brief evaluation with no reference to improvement.
