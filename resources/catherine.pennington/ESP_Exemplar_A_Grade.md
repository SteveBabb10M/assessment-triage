# ESP A-Grade Exemplar — Tasks 1.1 and 1.2 (Summer 2025)

**Source:** City & Guilds 8715-033 Employer-Set Project, Summer 2025 A grade exemplar (v1.0)
**Brief context:** IdeaHub — a marketing and advertising company in Birmingham (9 years established, ~17 employees across multiple teams). Problem: lack of cohesion and communication between teams, missed deadlines, frustrated employees. Budget: £200,000 over 6 months. Goal: stand out to a new client by demonstrating ability to hit deadlines, produce quality products, ensure value for money, and show continuous improvement.

## Task 1.1 Exemplar Summary

### What the candidate produced:

**PESTLE Analysis** — Six clearly headed sections covering:
- Political: UK tax rates (54.5% top marginal rate on corporate profits cited), Labour government business growth initiatives, corporation tax cap at 25%
- Economic: near-recession economy, Trump tariff impacts, UK GDP stagnation post-COVID (specific figures cited: £2.5bn vs £2.2bn)
- Social: public unrest, strikes, cost of living pressures, but marketing industry well-paid (76% salary increase statistic cited)
- Technological: AI adoption (2% current, 10% projected), communication software options, impact on creative industries
- Legal: Fair Work Act, employment law, Data Protection Act
- Environmental: transportation carbon footprint (17% of emissions), power usage in digital businesses

Each factor was explicitly linked back to how it would affect IdeaHub and influence the solution choice.

**SWOT Analysis** — Four-quadrant format:
- Strengths: 9 years stable, multiple managers and teams, new hires, clear objectives
- Weaknesses: poor inter-team communication, missed deadlines, web dev team vs client disagreements
- Opportunities: UK is Europe's largest marketing market, 70% digital, client networking/recommendations, 49.4% of businesses fail in first 5 years (so IdeaHub's longevity is competitive)
- Threats: small team, Birmingham's proximity to Manchester (saturated market), weak economy

**Wider Research** — Three additional sections:
- Structure, People and Management: hierarchical structure analysis, link to communication failures
- Digital Technology and Tools: web dev capabilities, need for communication software
- Business Processes and Procedures: identified communication as the key driver of all problems

**Research Outcomes Summary Table** — Linked SWOT and PESTLE findings to the project, drawing conclusions about what the research means for the solution

**Two Solutions:**
1. Training (Kaizen + communication skills) + new communication software (Connecteam) — £134,000
2. AI implementation + comprehensible task management training for managers — £179,000

**Cost Benefit Analysis** — Table format with costs, benefits, and negatives for each solution. Included specific costings (£1,000 per employee × 17 × 2 courses = £34,000 training; £100,000 software). Environmental considerations included.

**Selected Solution:** Solution 1 (training + software) — justified by ticking all boxes, leaving £66,000 contingency, lower environmental impact, directly addressing root communication problems.

### Key Strengths of This Exemplar (Task 1.1):
- Consistent use of external sources with URLs cited
- PESTLE factors explicitly linked to the project context
- Quantitative data used throughout (statistics, costings, GDP figures)
- Two genuinely different solutions rather than variations of the same idea
- Cost benefit analysis included advantages, disadvantages, AND negatives
- Selected solution justified with clear reasoning linking back to brief requirements
- Environmental considerations woven into solution comparison

### Areas Where It Lost Marks:
- Planning approach scored 4/6 — suggests the organisation/structure of research could have been more methodical and thorough
- Research could have included more non-traditional sources

## Task 1.2 Exemplar Summary

### What the candidate produced:

A complete PID using the template structure with all sections completed:

**Project Information:** "Improving Cohesion Through Training and New Software" — dated, authored, version 1.1

**Background and Scope:** Clear description of what the project is (ensuring IdeaHub stands out to the client), what it will involve (Kaizen training, communication training, new software), and who it will affect (all employees). Distinguished between direct project impacts and wider organisational benefits.

**Project Rationale:** Listed the specific problems the project addresses: poor communication between teams, late feedback, project scrapping, processes not followed, unclear time allocation, missed internal deadlines. Explained why this pathway was chosen — it doesn't change the business's core fundamentals, leaving room for future innovation.

**Project Aims and Objectives:** Main aim: eradicate lack of cohesion and communication. Objective: increased productivity and work rate by project end. Detailed the sequencing — software first (longest lead time), then communication training, then Kaizen training. Justified the ordering (software first because it takes longest to embed, and if it goes wrong early there's time to fix it).

**Budget:** Total £200,000. Breakdown: £34,000 training, £100,000 software installation and training, £66,000 contingency. Clear about what contingency covers.

**Project Management Team:** Table showing roles and reporting lines across the organisation.

**Project Key Stakeholders:** Named individuals with specific roles and responsibilities:
- Client Service Manager as Project Manager
- Managing Director for major decisions
- Training Provider (external)
- Software Provider (ConnectTeam)
- Project Coordinator
- Operations Manager
- Employees (testing software, reporting feedback)
- Customers (feedback for progress measurement)

**Project Deliverables:** Three deliverables with format and target dates:
- Communication Software — 25/06/2025
- Communication Training — 30/07/2025
- Kaizen Training — 30/08/2025

**Risks:** Five risks identified with level and mitigation:
1. Resistance to Change (High) — give employees a voice, show benefits
2. Software installation issues (Medium) — backup budget, try to resolve without spending first
3. Employee absences (Medium) — remote training and notes
4. Sudden change of management (Low) — priority on catching up new management
5. Training provider delays (Medium) — additional time built in before deadline

**Quality Management Method:** Kaizen philosophy for continuous improvement; oversight of training sessions with evaluation; consideration of employee numbers per session.

**Project Evaluation Method:** Comparison of before/after statistics (team interactions, deadlines met); employee feedback on software and training; £66,000 contingency available for changes if evaluation shows problems.

### Key Strengths of This Exemplar (Task 1.2):
- Comprehensive coverage of all PID sections
- Clear logical sequencing of deliverables with justified ordering
- Risk register with graduated severity levels and specific mitigations
- Stakeholder identification included both internal and external parties
- Budget breakdown clearly linked to project components
- Evaluation method included both quantitative metrics and qualitative feedback
- Scope clearly defined — what the project will and won't change

### Areas Where It Lost Marks:
- Scored 7/8 on planning and 7/9 on knowledge/skills — very strong but not full marks
- Evaluation method could have been more concrete (acknowledged by candidate in their own Task 2.2 evaluation)
